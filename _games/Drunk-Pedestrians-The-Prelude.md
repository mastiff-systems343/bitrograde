Six carnival games, one derelict space station, zero working gravity generators.

Your only guide is a musician who hasn't been sober since the last docking bay depressurized — an unreliable narrator with a trumpet case full of loose change and no memory of how he got here. He remembers the smell of funnel cake. He remembers a ring toss. He does not remember his name.

Each booth floats free of the last, tethered by cable and momentum. Land wrong and the whole rig lurches, flipping "down" into some other direction entirely. The milk bottles don't stack so much as orbit. The duck shoot ducks don't so much waddle as drift. Somewhere in the wreckage is a jackpot nobody's claimed, and a reason nobody's come back for it.

Six games. One drunk. No up.
